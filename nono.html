About

Davor Banovic Davor started writing reasonably early in his life as probably most writers did. He wrote several short stories and poems. Most of it got published most of it in mid nineties. However, with the dawn of 21st century he almost completely stopped writing. But, fortunately or not, he is back. Currently, he is pivoting to a nonfiction direction. His major interests are related to the Bible studies, 18th century to modern philosophy and philosophy of language. He finds most enjoyment in writings of St. Paul, Arthur Schopenhauer, George Berkeley, Richard Rorty, Leonardo Boff, David Bohm and Jacques Lacan. Influence of all those are visible in his works where he is trying to contribute to a development of a fragile culture of dialogue. Davor is holdig a masters degree in theology. He is employed in a software start-up so he knows to appreciate that little free time he has. He is happily married.
